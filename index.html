Holy Smokes!

This inspirational book features life lessons from the university’s late sports chaplain Fr. James Riehle, who helped players and coaches overcome adversity and become successful on and off the athletic field.

Each chapter features a different way to improve your life as if Notre Dame’s championship chaplain, who won four national football titles with three different head football coaches, was alive today to inspire you to fulfill your mission.

About the Author

  •  
    Credentials

    Author, Paul Dykewicz is a seasoned beat reporter, editor, columnist and commentator who has worked for a variety of local and national news organizations including the Wall Street Journal. A winner of numerous journalism awards, he works in Washington, D.C., and lives in Rockville, Maryland.

    Schedule Me Here
  •  
    Appearances

    Paul makes appearances which give him a chance to highlight the wit and wisdom of Notre Dame's late sports chaplain, Fr. James Riehle. His "Holy Smokes!" book, with a Foreword by Lou Holtz, is inspirational, motivational and biographical, with each chapter featuring a different way to improve your life based on advice given by Fr. Riehle to student-athletes, coaches, administrators, professors, and others for 40-plus years.

  •  
    Endorsements

    Endorsements and stories for the book come from Notre Dame players and coaches of various faiths, races and backgrounds. They include former Washington Redskins’ Joe Theismann and Reggie Brooks, four-time Super Bowl winner Joe Montana, “Rocket” Ismail, “Digger” Phelps, Ara Parseghian and Fighting Irish basketball coach and Rockville native Mike Brey. Many thanks to national title-winning football coach Lou Holtz for providing the powerful Foreword, which explained anyone who thinks a chaplain does not make much difference did not know Fr. Riehle.

  •  
    Charities

    Outside of work, Paul volunteers with a faith-based organization to assist the poor in Southeast Washington, D.C., to learn personal finance skills needed to lift themselves out of debt. The program is one of the many worthwhile community-based initiatives of Catholic Charities of the Archdiocese Washington.
